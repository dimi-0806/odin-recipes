Sinigang

“Sinigang na baboy” by Anne Molina is licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Description

I love sinigang. It's my ultimate comfort food. From its preparation to finally eating it, every step brings me a sense of calm, joy, and the feeling of home.

Sinigang is a classic Filipino dish known for its signature sour flavor. It can be made using different souring agents like tamarind, guava, kamias, or green mango. There are also packaged sinigang mixes that make cooking easier and more convenient. You can enjoy it with various proteins too, such as pork, fish, or shrimp.

In this recipe, I am using pork spare ribs, but any cut of pork works just fine. For the souring agent, I use a packet of sinigang mix because it's readily available in stores near me. But if you prefer using fresh ingredients, check out Panlasang Pinoy's Sinigang recipe, which uses fresh tamarind as the souring agent.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork spare ribs
  • 1 bunch of kangkong or water spinach, leaves separated and stem cut into 2 1/2 to 3-inch lengths
  • 1 onion, peeled and quartered
  • 2 large tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 eggplant, ends trimmed and sliced to 1/2-inch thick rounds
  • 1 radish, peeled and sliced to 1/2-inch thick rounds
  • 1/2 bunch string beans, ends trimmed and cut into 2 1/2 to 3-inch lengths
  • 2 long green chilis
  • 8 cups water
  • 1-2 packets sinigang mix
  • 5-8 peppercorns, freshly crushed
  • vegetable oil
  • fish sauce to taste
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Rinse the pork ribs and drain well. Set aside.
  2. In a pot over medium heat, add vegetable oil.
  3. Once the oil is hot, add the onions and saute until slightly translucent.
  4. Add the tomatoes and saute until softened. You can also char the tomatoes slighlty if desired.
  5. Add the ribs, freshly crushed peppercorns, and around 1 tablespoon of fish sauce. Cook until all the sides of the meat have a nice golden brown crust.
  6. Add enough water to cover everything in the pot and bring to a boil.
  7. After a few minutes of boiling, lower heat and simmer for about an hour or until the meat is tender. Add more water as necessary.
  8. Add 1-2 packets of sinigang mix, depending on your preference, and stir until completely dissolved.
  9. Add the long green chilis, radish, and eggplant. You may also add the string beans at this point if your prefer them to be soft. Conitnue to simmer for about 5-6 minutes.
  10. If you prefer the string beans to be a bit crisp, add them at this point. Coninute to simmer for about 2-3 minutes.
  11. Taste the soup. Adjust the taste to your liking with fish sauce or salt and pepper.
  12. Add the kangkong stems and leaves. Cover the pot and turn off the heat. Let the residual heat cook the kangkong for about 3-5 minutes.
  13. Transfer the sinigang to a bowl and serve with rice.
