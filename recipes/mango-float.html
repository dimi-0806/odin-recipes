Mango Float

“Mango Float (Crema de Mangga) - Philippines” by Obsidian Soul is licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Description

Mango float is one of my favorite desserts and my go-to recipe when we have mangoes at home. It is a no-bake Filipino icebox cake made of layers of biscuit (traditionally, Graham crackers), whipped cream, and ripe mangoes. It is very easy to prepare and tastes absolutely delicious.

Tip: Chill everything beforehand, especially the cream, condensed milk, mixing bowl, and the whisk attachments of your mixer. This helps the cream mixture reach stable stiff peaks.

Ingredients

  • ripe mangoes
  • Graham crackers (or any biscuit of your choice)
  • heavy cream, chilled
  • condensed milk, chilled

Steps

  1. Prepare a baking dish.
  2. Peel the mangoes and cut the cheeks off as close to the seed as possible.
  3. Cut the mango cheeks into thin slices. Set aside.
  4. In a chilled bowl, combine the cream and condensed milk. Whisk until stiff peaks form. Use a stand mixer or hand mixer to make this step easier.
  5. Place a small amount of the whipped cream at the bottom of the baking dish and spread it evenly. This will keep your biscuits from moving around during assembly.
  6. Add a single layer of Graham crackers.
  7. Top the Graham crackers with a layer of the cream mixture. Spread it evenly with an offset spatula or spoon.
  8. Add a layer of sliced mangoes.
  9. Repeat steps 6-8 to make another layer. Finish with a layer of the cream mixture.
  10. For the final layer, decorate the dessert as desired. You may add a layer of crushed grahams or arranged the mango slices decoratively.
  11. Cover the dish with cling wrap and place it in the fridge to chill overnight.
  12. Once chilled, cut the dessert into squares and serve.
